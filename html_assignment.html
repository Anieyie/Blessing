Nigerian Fried Rice

This colorful and flavor-packed Nigerian Fried Rice is a party favorite. Filled with fresh vegetables, liver, and seasoned rice, it’s a staple for festive occasions and special gatherings in Nigeria.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 40 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pre-cooked long grain rice
  • 1 cup diced liver (boiled and fried)
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 cup sweet corn
  • 1 cup chopped green beans
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chicken stock for flavor

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and garlic; sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in carrots, green beans, green peas, and sweet corn; cook for 3–5 minutes.
  4. Add diced liver and stir well.
  5. Sprinkle in curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper; mix to combine.
  6. Pour in a little chicken stock to enhance the flavor.
  7. Add the pre-cooked rice and stir-fry for about 5 minutes until well combined and heated through.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot.

Tips

For a richer flavor, use freshly made chicken stock instead of water, and ensure the vegetables remain slightly crunchy for the best texture.
